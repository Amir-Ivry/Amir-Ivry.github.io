I study what I call the broken compass problem in speech systems—when evaluation is ambiguous and misdirects research, slowing progress instead of accelerating it.

As a postdoctoral researcher, my work seeks to rebuild that compass: making speech, music, and audio systems more reliable by aligning evaluation with human perception.

Over the past decade, I’ve had the privilege of working across both academia and industry. At Microsoft, I most recently served as a Principal Researcher and Head of Data Domain in the Teams organization. Along the way I co-led the NOTSOFAR-1 Challenge and benchmark, won the global Microsoft Hackathon with an accent-softening solution for Teams, and co-authored research on diarization and ASR.

Parallel to my industry tenure, I pursued a direct-track PhD in Electrical and Computer Engineering at the Technion under the supervision of Prof. Israel Cohen. Outside of research, I find balance in nature—a space where many of my ideas take shape.
